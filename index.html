01
Healthcare ops · prescriptive

ed-twin — a digital twin that ranks the levers cutting ED length of stay

A discrete-event simulation of emergency-department patient flow (arrival, triage, physician assessment, admission, boarding) finds the binding constraint is physician capacity, not bed count. One added physician cuts the median 467-minute stay by roughly five hours under heavy load; five added beds produce no measurable change. Run across 30 seeded replications at 95% confidence, deployed as a live app on a precomputed grid. MIMIC-IV-ED calibration in progress.

02
RevOps & marketing · predictive

Customer segmentation that found the revenue was one purchase from churning

RFM scoring and K-Means across 93,349 e-commerce customers and $15.4M in revenue. Two churn-vulnerable segments, Potential Loyalists and At Risk, drive 59% of that revenue, while only 0.25% of customers show any repeat-purchase loyalty signal. I demonstrated model-selection judgment by rejecting a non-converging probabilistic CLV model for a defensible deterministic one, and wrote that up as a finding.

03
Supply chain · descriptive

The premium shipping tier that cost 2.4× and delivered worst

An end-to-end pipeline over 12,000 shipments across five warehouses and four carriers, surfacing $87K in late-delivery penalties on a 38.4% on-time rate. The signature anomaly: the premium carrier cost 2.4 times more per shipment yet delivered on time only 10% of the time, six times worse than the cheapest carrier, a clear renegotiation target. Modeled through PostgreSQL staging and analytical marts, delivered as a 3-page Power BI dashboard.
