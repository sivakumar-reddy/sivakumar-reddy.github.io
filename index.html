Healthcare ops · Prescriptive simulation

ED Twin, a digital twin that ranks the levers on ED stay

A discrete event simulation of emergency department patient flow, calibrated on 424,725 real encounters, that finds the binding constraint is physician capacity, not bed count. One added physician cuts the median 467 minute stay by roughly five hours under heavy load; five added beds produce no measurable change. Deployed as a live interactive app.

RevOps & marketing · Predictive

Segmentation that found the revenue was one purchase from churning

RFM scoring and K-Means across 93,349 e-commerce customers and $15.4M in revenue. Two churn vulnerable segments drive 59% of that revenue, while only 0.25% of customers show any repeat purchase loyalty signal. I made a defensible model selection call, rejecting a non converging probabilistic CLV model for a deterministic one, and wrote it up as a finding.

Supply chain · Descriptive BI

The premium shipping tier that cost 2.4× and delivered worst

An end to end pipeline over 12,000 shipments across five warehouses and four carriers, surfacing $87K in late delivery penalties on a 38.4% on time rate. The premium carrier cost 2.4 times more per shipment yet delivered on time only 10% of the time, a clear renegotiation target. Modeled through PostgreSQL staging and analytical marts, delivered as a Power BI dashboard.
